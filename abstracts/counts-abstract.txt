Many real-world time series problems require more than pattern matching; they demand multi-step reasoning, domain knowledge, and unstructured context. Reinforcement learning (RL) enables large language models (LLMs) to produce effective chain-of-thought reasoning in math and code, but LLMs remain weak on numerical time series due to limited perception, despite time series tasks being inherently verifiable. We introduce COUNTS: Contextualized Outcome-Guided Understanding of Numerical Time Series, a framework that equips a pretrained LLM to reason explicitly over diverse time series tasks using RL from verifiable rewards. COUNTS learns a high-fidelity discrete time series representation via a residual vector-quantized variational autoencoder, yielding tokens that integrate seamlessly with the LLM vocabulary. The LLM is then trained in two stages: supervised fine-tuning on interleaved time-series and text with context and instructions, followed by reinforcement learning on time series tasks, where verifiable rewards are computed from task labels and a structured format. Across multiple benchmarks, this RL stage improves performance over supervised baselines by 6-19 percentage points, surpassing both frontier LLMs and domain-specific methods.
